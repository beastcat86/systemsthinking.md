System Thinking

A system is how different parts work together and connect to be able to understand that. Instead of looking at a bunch of pieces it is important to figure out how those pieces work together. For example, a PC needs multiple parts to work to be able to turn on and function and if one of those parts are missing the PC may be slow or not turn on. “Systems engineering realizes successful systems. Understanding, communication and complexity” (P.85, Holt).

What is system thinking?

System thinking compared to linear thinking is linear thinking like for example on my PlayStation if my game isn’t downloading because I have no more storage I just go and uninstall my other games to make it work but, if I was to use system thinking I would try to figure out other ways the storage may be getting taken up like gaming clips I have saved or purchasing more storage for my PlayStation. Another example is in a workplace blaming one specific department for why something isn’t working and instead solving the real problem is every step that led to that problem occurring.

More about system thinking

According to an article I read from MIT from Professor Crawley Systems thinking is used to see how teams work, on devices, tracking information and processes “those skills include being able to engage in the unknown and think differently about the relationships between the parts that make up a system” (Crawley, 2022). It is to be able to recognize that a change can affect an entire system and that all parts need to work together.

How parts work together

In an article I read by Ross Arnold and John Wade explains that “the use of systems thinking transcends many disciplines, support and connecting them in intuitive but highly impactful ways” System thinking sees that there is a cause to everything and all the parts that contributed to that cause if my phone is slow is it because it hasn’t been update is it because I have to many apps open there is always a cause to why something is occurring. In the article by Ross Arnold and John Wade you could have the best of security but it’s not just about the security it’s about every other part that goes into the entire system.

More explaination

An article by SNHU explains there are multiple concepts within system thinking “boundaries, feedback loops, interdependence and open and closed systems” (Morganelli, 2024). Having a system is never about being the correct person it “helps each person manage different perspectives to bring them together in the best way” (Morganelli, 2024). In a professional setting it’s about all the roles that play into the team not just the billing department or the marketing department it is every team member.
